GERD (Gastroesophageal Reflux Disease)

  • PUD: H. pylori infection, NSAID use, excessive acid production.
  • IBD: Genetic predisposition, autoimmune factors, environmental triggers.
  • IBS: Altered gut motility, visceral hypersensitivity, bacterial overgrowth.
  • Celiac Disease: Genetic susceptibility, gluten consumption.
  • Diverticular Disease: Low fiber diet, age-related changes in the colon wall.

Weak lower esophageal sphincter, hiatal hernia, obesity, pregnancy.

  • GERD: Heartburn, regurgitation, chest pain, chronic cough
  • PUD: Abdominal pain, nausea, vomiting, gastrointestinal bleeding.
  • IBD: Diarrhea, abdominal pain, weight loss, fatigue, malnutrition.
  • IBS: Abdominal pain, bloating, constipation, diarrhea.
  • Celiac Disease: Diarrhea, bloating, weight loss, malabsorption of nutrients.
  • Diverticular Disease: Abdominal pain, bloating, changes in bowel habits, complications like diverticulitis.

Heartburn, regurgitation, chest pain, chronic cough.

  • GERD: Avoid trigger foods (e.g., spicy foods, caffeine), smaller meals, elevate head during sleep.
  • PUD: Helicobacter pylori eradication therapy, avoid NSAIDs, eat regular meals, limit alcohol and caffeine.
  • IBD: Maintain adequate nutrition, avoid trigger foods, consider low-residue diet during flare-ups, possibly trial of specific carbohydrate diet (SCD) or other diets.
  • IBS: High-fiber diet, low-FODMAP diet, probiotics, regular meals, stress management.
  • Celiac Disease: Strict gluten-free diet, educate on hidden sources of gluten, monitor nutrient deficiencies (iron, calcium, B vitamins).
  • Diverticular Disease: High-fiber diet, adequate hydration, avoid seeds and nuts during acute phases.

Avoid trigger foods (spicy, caffeine), smaller meals, elevate head during sleep.

  • GERD: Proton pump inhibitors (PPIs) like omeprazole, H2 blockers like ranitidine, antacids.
  • PUD: PPIs, antibiotics (for H. pylori), cytoprotective agents (sucralfate).
  • IBD: Aminosalicylates, corticosteroids (for acute flare-ups), immunomodulators (e.g., azathioprine), biologic therapies (e.g., infliximab).
  • IBS: Antispasmodics (e.g., dicyclomine), antidepressants (for pain modulation), bulking agents (e.g., psyllium).
  • Celiac Disease: Strict gluten-free diet (no medications specifically for treatment).
  • Diverticular Disease: Antibiotics (if diverticulitis occurs), pain management medications as needed.

Proton pump inhibitors (PPIs) like omeprazole, H2 blockers like ranitidine, antacids.

Peptic Ulcer Disease (PUD)

H. pylori infection, NSAID use, excessive acid production.

Abdominal pain, nausea, vomiting, gastrointestinal bleeding.

Helicobacter pylori eradication therapy, avoid NSAIDs, eat regular meals.

PPIs, antibiotics (for H. pylori), cytoprotective agents (sucralfate).

Inflammatory Bowel Disease (IBD)

Genetic predisposition, autoimmune factors, environmental triggers.

Diarrhea, abdominal pain, weight loss, fatigue, malnutrition.

Maintain adequate nutrition, avoid trigger foods, consider specific carbohydrate diet (SCD).

Aminosalicylates, corticosteroids (for acute flare-ups), immunomodulators, biologic therapies.
